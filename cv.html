Preliminary Title “Robust and agile control of rocket propulsion systems”
Autonomy is becoming ever more important for modern spaceflight and upcoming missions to remote locations. Hence, my PhD focuses on the intersection of rocketry, control and sometimes (ChatGPT: find a better word than sometimes that says: I am using it from time to time but pls don’t expect to be an expert) machine learning. In particular, I leverage modern control paradigms – such as MPC – to allow for more agile and more robust thrust control of rocket propulsion systems, with the goal of maintaining control authority over the system even in the event of faults. Sounds interesting? Feel free to check my publications.
Developing strong control theory is great, but it also must work in practice. This is why we launched the ASCENT project, with the goal of building a free-flying vertical take-off and vertical landing platform. The propulsion system of ASCENT will serve me as a test setup to verify my control research.
July 2025 – September 2025
Leuven, Belgium

Visiting PhD – Robotics & Control

Katholieke Universiteit Leuven, MECO Research Lab
Advisors: Prof. Jan Swevers, Alvaro Martinez
My academic visit to Leuven allowed me to work on a very exciting and different control problem from my everyday life: the time-optimal motion of an overhead crane with collision avoidance. I implemented an Artificial Reference MPC (developed by a colleague in Leuven) for the nonlinear overhead crane dynamics and augmented the control policy with a higher-order control barrier function to ensure collision avoidance. This approach, together with the FATROP solver developed at MECO, allowed me to demonstrate the effectiveness of the control architecture through real-world experiments. Details will be available in the publication at CCTA 2026.

Darmstadt, Germany

BSc – Mechanical and Process Engineering

Technical University of Darmstadt
GPA: 1.67
BSc Thesis: “3D-CFD geometry- and flow field analysis of a centrifugal compressor database through correlation functions”

I analysed patterns within large CFD data bases (>2000 CFD runs) of centrifugal compressors. The data base was a result of a CFD-based design optimization of the centrifugal stage. I was implementing a Python tool that allows to compute correlations between changes in the flow field / geometry of the pump and performance metrics such as the isentropic efficiency of the compressor. This way I was able to identify patterns and sensitive regions in the flow field that are most relevant to the performance metrics considered.
